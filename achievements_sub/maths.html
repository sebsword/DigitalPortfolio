UK Maths Challenge

In addition to GCSE maths which we were completing, every year since year 7 we have been entered for the UK maths challenge atleast once. The maths challenge itself at all levels is regulated by the Oxford asset mangement team and is recognised as one of the most challenging mathematics awards that can be taken before sixth-form and is an extremely valued award.

My Exam

Although I took the exam several times throughout my time at fulham college boys' school/henry compton my first majour achievement was in year 11, when I was awarded the bronze certificate, to put into perspective at this point I was consistantly achieving A*s in all of my mathematics mock exams, this only serves as a testamant to the unrivalled rigor and challenge the award poses.

Political Career

The maths challenge is seperated into three seperate awards, junior , intermediate and senior where bronze silver and gold certificates are won.

The Awards

  • Junior Challenge: Aimed at pupils in who are in year 8 or below.
  • Intermediate Challenge: Aimed at pupils who are in year 11, it is the exam I took and lasts one hour.
  • Senior Challenge: Aimed at 16-19 year olds in sixth-form / college.
